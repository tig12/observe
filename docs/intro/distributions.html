Distributions

Distributions handled by this program generate two kinds of arrays: one dimension array and two dimensions array (a table).

One-dimensional arrays - 1dim

These are simple arrays which permit to store ordered sets of values.
These arrays can be simple regular arrays, where the keys to access to values are integers starting with 0, or associative arrays(also called maps or hash tables), where the keys can be strings.
This kind of array is abbreviated 1dim

Here are the distributions stored in 1dim arrays:

Distributions by year

+------------+-------------+------------+-------------+
|    1891    |     1892    |     ...    |     2025    |
+------------+-------------+------------+-------------+
|   177283   |   187633    |     ...    |    20302    |
+------------+-------------+------------+-------------+
Means that there are 177283 dates in 1891 etc.

Distributions by day

+------------+-------------+------------+-------------+
|    01-01   |    01-02    |     ...    |     12-31   |
+------------+-------------+------------+-------------+
|   183547   |   187633    |     ...    |   182667    |
+------------+-------------+------------+-------------+

Planetary positions

We have one such distribution per planet: 
+------------+-------------+------------+-------------+
|      0     |      1      |     ...    |     359     |
+------------+-------------+------------+-------------+
|   145265   |   143657    |     ...    |   148775    |
+------------+-------------+------------+-------------+
Means that there are 145265 dates with the longitude of the planet between 0 and 1° etc.

Aspects

We have one such distribution per couple of planets: 
+------------+-------------+------------+-------------+
|      0     |      1      |     ...    |     359     |
+------------+-------------+------------+-------------+
|   159843   |   155412    |     ...    |   149553    |
+------------+-------------+------------+-------------+
Means that there are 159843 dates with the longitude of the planet between 0 and 1° etc.

Two-dimensional arrays - 2dim

These are arrays which permit to store a table or a two-dimensional array. 
      +------------+-------------+------------+-------------+
      |      0     |      1      |     ...    |     359     |
+-----+------------+-------------+------------+-------------+
|  0  |   159843   |   155412    |     ...    |   149553    |
+-----+------------+-------------+------------+-------------+
| ... |    ...     |     ...     |     ...    |   149553    |
+-----+------------+-------------+------------+-------------+
| 359 |     ...    |     ...     |     ...    |   149553    |
+-----+------------+-------------+------------+-------------+

These arrays could be associative arrays, but currently the program only handles regular arrays (indexed 0, 1, ...).
This kind of array is abbreviated 2dim.

Example

Two views for the same distribution

1dim and 2dim arrays can be used to represent a distribution.

If you look at the "death in France" study and the distribution comparing the position of mars at birth and mars at death: These two images represent the same data. The first image uses a 1dim array, and the second a 2dim array.
The first image is less precise: it only shows the distribution of the angles between the two planets, but it doesn't show where these angles come from.
The second image shows the distribution of the positions of the two planets.
