Questions

This software is at a draft stage, I use this development to learn to perform statistical tests on astrology. I'd like to perform rigourous and valid tests.
But my knowledge and understanding of statistics is quite limited.
So I listed here the main questions raised by this development.
Some questions are related to the particular case of the deaths in France study.

Cleaning the data

The the deaths in France study is based on data. The import process permits eliminate 0.14 % of erroneous dates (113 677 out of 28 917 511).
But the day of birth distribution shows a clearly suspect excess of biths on January 1st.
How can I fix this ? Should I compute the mean value between December 31th and January 2nd and randomly suppress records to reach this mean ?

Control groups

Are control groups necessary ?

Control groups are computed to build the "expected" data, the data we should theoretically observe.
But the method described by Didier Castille in his article "A Link between Birth and Death" ("Un Lien entre la Naissance et le Décès") permits to compute theoretical data without control groups.
So can we perform valid tests without control groups ?

How many control groups should be computed ?

Is there a rule or a formula to know the number of control groups to compute ?

How to compute control groups ?

Current method just loops on birth dates, and randomly selects another person to take his / her death date. The choice of a computation method is arbitrary, and it affects the resulting control groups, which may affect the results of statistical tests. So this question seems very important to correctly perform an analysis.
